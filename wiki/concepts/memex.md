# Memex

**Definition.** A hypothetical personal knowledge device proposed by [[wiki/entities/vannevar-bush]] in his 1945 essay *As We May Think*. Bush described a desk-sized machine that would store all of a person's books, records, and communications, and — crucially — allow the user to build **associative trails** linking documents together, anticipating hypertext by decades.

## Why it matters

The Memex is the direct conceptual ancestor of the [[wiki/concepts/llm-wiki-pattern]]. Both share the same core bet: **the value isn't in the documents; it's in the connections between them**, actively curated by (or for) a single user.

## Key properties

- **Private and personal.** Not a public web — a user's own curated collection.[[wiki/sources/2026-04-17-llm-wiki-pattern]]
- **Associative trails.** The user builds explicit links between documents, creating navigable paths of related material. These trails are first-class artifacts.[[wiki/sources/2026-04-17-llm-wiki-pattern]]
- **Connections over content.** The links matter as much as the documents themselves.[[wiki/sources/2026-04-17-llm-wiki-pattern]]
- **Unsolved: maintenance.** Bush never answered _who builds and maintains the trails_. The answer the [[wiki/concepts/llm-wiki-pattern]] offers is: the LLM does.[[wiki/sources/2026-04-17-llm-wiki-pattern]]

## Related concepts

- Direct descendant: [[wiki/concepts/llm-wiki-pattern]] — resurrects the Memex vision with LLMs handling maintenance.
- Contrast with [[wiki/concepts/rag]] — RAG is stateless retrieval; the Memex (and the LLM Wiki) are about accumulated, curated structure.
