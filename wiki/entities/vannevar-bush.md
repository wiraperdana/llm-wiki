# Vannevar Bush

American engineer, inventor, and science administrator (1890–1974). Best known in this wiki as the originator of the [[wiki/concepts/memex]] — a hypothetical personal knowledge device proposed in his 1945 essay *As We May Think*, which foreshadowed hypertext and personal computing.

## Key facts

- Directed the U.S. Office of Scientific Research and Development during WWII, overseeing projects including the Manhattan Project's research coordination.
- Published *"As We May Think"* in _The Atlantic_, July 1945 — the essay that introduced the Memex concept.[[wiki/sources/2026-04-17-llm-wiki-pattern]]
- Envisioned the Memex as a desk-sized device that stored a person's books, records, and communications, and let the user build "associative trails" between documents — prefiguring hypertext by decades.[[wiki/sources/2026-04-17-llm-wiki-pattern]]

## Relationships

- Conceptualized [[wiki/concepts/memex]] — the direct intellectual ancestor of the [[wiki/concepts/llm-wiki-pattern]].

## Notes

The LLM Wiki pattern explicitly positions itself as closer to Bush's original vision than the World Wide Web ended up being: **private, actively curated, with connections between documents as valuable as the documents themselves**. The missing ingredient in Bush's vision was who would do the maintenance — the claim is that LLMs fill that role.[[wiki/sources/2026-04-17-llm-wiki-pattern]]
